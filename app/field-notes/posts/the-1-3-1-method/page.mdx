If someone on your team keeps escalating problems without owning the solution, try giving them a constraint before the next conversation.

The 1-3-1 method: come back with **one clear problem statement**, **three options you've considered**, and **one recommendation**. Not a list of concerns. Not a question that's really a request for you to decide. A recommendation — theirs.

It sounds almost too simple. But the act of forcing someone to generate options and then pick one changes the dynamic entirely. Most of the time, people already know what they'd do. They just haven't been asked to commit to it out loud.

I've used this with engineers who were stuck in analysis paralysis, with leads who wanted cover before making a call, and with directs who had learned (from previous managers) that surfacing a problem was the same as solving it. It works in all three cases for the same reason: it moves accountability back to the person closest to the work, which is where it belongs.

One thing to watch: if someone comes back and all three options are obviously bad, that's useful signal too. Either the problem statement isn't right, or they need more context before they can own the decision. Either way, you've learned something.
