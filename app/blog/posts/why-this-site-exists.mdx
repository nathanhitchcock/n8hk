publishedAt: '2025-10-26'

---


There’s a strange clarity that comes from finally slowing down long enough to listen to your own thoughts.  
For years, I’ve worked at the intersection of people, automation, and AI — building systems meant to scale support and reliability. But in chasing efficiency, I noticed how easy it is to lose the human rhythm that made the work meaningful in the first place.

This site is my way of finding that rhythm again. It’s a place to explore the intersection of humans, automation, and AI — not through press releases or polished frameworks, but through honest reflection. I want to understand how empathy and design shape the way we collaborate with intelligent systems, and what it means to build technology that empowers instead of replaces.

Over the past decade, I’ve led teams through all kinds of transformations — reorganizations, automation pushes, AI integrations, the slow rewiring of how work gets done. Every one of those transitions revealed something deeper about the relationship between humans and the systems we create. At their best, the tools disappear and what’s left is flow, clarity, and connection. At their worst, they amplify everything that’s broken.

I think we’re entering a new chapter — one where technology doesn’t just *do* things for us, but begins to *work* with us. And I don’t want to just watch that happen; I want to understand it as it unfolds. Writing, for me, is how I think through those questions out loud.

So this is where I’ll do that.  
Sometimes the posts here will read like essays or RFDs — small, open-ended explorations that build toward something bigger over time. Other times, they’ll just be fragments or ideas I haven’t fully figured out yet. Either way, the goal isn’t to produce polished answers, but to stay curious about the future we’re building, and to make sense of what it means to be human inside it.
