Career growth stalls when communication lags behind technical skill.

## Objective

Develop communication that improves decision quality and execution speed.

## High-Leverage Communication Patterns

- Context first: what problem matters and why now.
- Decision framing: options, tradeoffs, recommendation.
- Update clarity: current status, next steps, next update.
- Audience adaptation: technical depth adjusted to the room.

## Practical Reps

- Write one design summary each sprint.
- Lead one architecture or incident recap each month.
- Practice concise updates in cross-functional channels.

## Signals of Progress

- Fewer misunderstanding loops.
- Faster decision cycles.
- Increased trust from peers and stakeholders.

<Callout title="Guardrails">
  <ul>
    <li>Do not hide behind technical jargon.</li>
    <li>Do not over-explain and lose the decision thread.</li>
    <li>Do not go silent when uncertainty appears.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>Great communication is a force multiplier for technical judgment.</p>
</Callout>

## Done Criteria

This step is complete when the engineer regularly drives clear technical decisions across mixed audiences.
