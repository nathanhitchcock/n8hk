Individual output has limits. Multiplication is how senior impact grows.

## Objective

Create force multipliers through mentorship, standards, and repeatable team practices.

## Multiplication Levers

- Mentor engineers through structured growth conversations.
- Turn implicit knowledge into explicit guides and templates.
- Improve engineering standards through examples and reviews.
- Raise quality bars without creating process drag.

## Practical Implementation

- Run recurring office hours or architecture clinics.
- Build one reusable playbook per quarter.
- Sponsor one engineer’s growth plan with measurable milestones.

## Signals of Progress

- Teammates unblock faster with your materials.
- Review quality improves team-wide.
- Critical knowledge is less person-dependent.

<Callout title="Guardrails">
  <ul>
    <li>Do not mentor by rewriting others' work.</li>
    <li>Do not create standards disconnected from real delivery pressure.</li>
    <li>Do not hoard context to stay indispensable.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>Principal-level impact compounds when others get stronger because you were there.</p>
</Callout>

## Done Criteria

This step is complete when the engineer’s presence measurably increases team capability and consistency.
