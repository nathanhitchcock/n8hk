Most execution failures are not effort failures. They are ownership failures.

Step 5 is about making accountability obvious: who owns outcomes, who decides, who contributes, and how conflicts escalate.

## Objective

Create clear ownership boundaries so work and decisions move without confusion or duplicate effort.

## Ownership Framework

For each active domain, define:

- Outcome owner: accountable for results.
- Decision owner: accountable for final call.
- Contributors: responsible for execution inputs.
- Escalation path: where unresolved conflicts go.

If ownership is shared by everyone, it is owned by no one.

## Decision Rights Matrix

Build a simple matrix by decision type:

- Team-level delivery decisions.
- Architecture/quality decisions.
- Stakeholder priority decisions.
- Scope and tradeoff decisions.

For each type, define decide/consult/inform explicitly.

## Clarify Interfaces

Ownership is not only internal. It must include adjacent teams.

Document:

- Handoff contracts.
- SLA expectations.
- Escalation contact points.

Cross-team friction drops when interface expectations are explicit.

## Common Failure Modes

Watch for:

- Phantom ownership: someone feels responsible but has no authority.
- Shadow ownership: unofficial decision makers override documented roles.
- Diffused ownership: too many approvers for small decisions.

Each of these slows the system.

<Callout title="Guardrails">
	<ul>
		<li>Do not weaponize ownership as blame.</li>
		<li>Do not centralize every decision to the manager.</li>
		<li>Do not let role clarity become organizational rigidity.</li>
	</ul>
	<p>Ownership should increase decision speed and learning, not fear.</p>
</Callout>

## Artifacts to Ship

By end of Step 5, publish:

1. Domain ownership map.
2. Decision rights matrix.
3. Escalation and interface playbook.

## Done Criteria

This step is complete when:

- Team members can explain who owns what without debate.
- Decision latency drops because authority is clear.
- Fewer work items stall due to unclear accountability.

Clear ownership is how reliability scales.
