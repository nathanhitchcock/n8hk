The path to principal requires influence beyond your reporting line.

## Objective

Drive alignment and execution across peers and partner teams without relying on formal authority.

## Influence Mechanics

- Build trust through consistency and technical credibility.
- Make coordination easier with clear proposals and scoped asks.
- Translate constraints across product, design, and engineering.
- Follow through relentlessly so commitments become believable.

## Influence Routines

- Share one cross-team technical memo monthly.
- Resolve one recurring team interface pain point each quarter.
- Practice stakeholder mapping before large initiatives.

## Signals of Progress

- Partners seek your input earlier.
- Cross-team dependencies move with less friction.
- Alignment happens faster during ambiguity.

<Callout title="Guardrails">
  <ul>
    <li>Do not mistake loudness for influence.</li>
    <li>Do not escalate before attempting direct alignment.</li>
    <li>Do not make commitments on behalf of teams without buy-in.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>Influence is earned through trust, clarity, and delivery.</p>
</Callout>

## Done Criteria

This step is complete when the engineer can lead cross-team execution through credibility and coordination.
