After you stabilize a team, the next move is not a reorg. It is diagnosis.

Most new managers inherit stories, not systems. Step 2 is about turning narratives into an evidence-based baseline.

## Objective

Build a one-page view of current reality so your next decisions are grounded in facts, not assumptions.

## What to Measure First

Measure only what helps you make better decisions in the next 30 days.

- Delivery flow: cycle time, blocked work, carryover.
- Quality: defect trends, rework frequency, escaped issues.
- Team health: workload balance, interruption rate, ownership clarity.
- Stakeholder friction: escalations, decision latency, expectation gaps.

## Build the Baseline Map

Create a baseline doc with four sections:

1. **Current outcomes**: what the team is producing and at what quality.
2. **Constraint map**: top recurring bottlenecks.
3. **Risk map**: where a miss would hurt most.
4. **Leverage map**: two to three fixes most likely to improve outcomes.

Keep this short enough that everyone can read it in five minutes.

## Run a Fast Evidence Loop

For each candidate issue, ask:

- Is this a one-off or a repeating pattern?
- What data supports it?
- Who owns the fix?
- What is the smallest intervention we can test in two weeks?

This keeps your team from debating opinions indefinitely.

## Artifacts to Ship

By end of this step, publish:

1. Team baseline one-pager.
2. Top 3 constraints list.
3. Two-week experiment plan with owners.

## Done Criteria

This step is complete when:

- Team and stakeholders agree on what is true.
- Priority constraints are named and owned.
- You have concrete experiments queued for Step 3.

A clear baseline turns reactive leadership into intentional leadership.
