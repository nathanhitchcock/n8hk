Principal engineers operate where organizational boundaries usually slow progress.

## Objective

Lead initiatives with cross-org dependencies and strategic importance.

## Cross-Org Execution Model

- Start with a shared problem definition and success criteria.
- Align on ownership model and decision rights.
- Sequence work to reduce critical path risk.
- Build transparent reporting for leadership and delivery teams.

## Operating Practices

- Publish a one-page initiative charter.
- Track dependency health weekly.
- Escalate with options, not just problems.

## Signals of Progress

- Multi-team programs ship with fewer deadline shocks.
- Stakeholders trust your risk framing.
- Strategic initiatives produce measurable outcomes.

<Callout title="Guardrails">
  <ul>
    <li>Do not launch cross-org work without explicit ownership boundaries.</li>
    <li>Do not bury tradeoffs to preserve superficial alignment.</li>
    <li>Do not let status reporting replace active risk management.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>Cross-org impact requires structure, not heroics.</p>
</Callout>

## Done Criteria

This step is complete when the engineer can reliably deliver high-impact initiatives across multiple teams.
