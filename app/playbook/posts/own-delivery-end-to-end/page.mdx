Principal engineers are trusted because they close loops, not because they write more code.

## Objective

Build end-to-end ownership: scope, execution, release, and operational follow-through.

## Ownership Behaviors

- Clarify the desired outcome before implementation.
- Break ambiguous work into milestones with risk notes.
- Coordinate dependencies early with explicit handoffs.
- Validate release readiness and post-release health.

## Delivery Checklist

- Problem statement is explicit.
- Tradeoffs are documented.
- Risks and rollback path are known.
- Stakeholders receive clear updates.

## Signals of Progress

- Work lands with fewer surprises.
- Stakeholder trust improves.
- Issues are surfaced early, not at deadline.

<Callout title="Guardrails">
  <ul>
    <li>Do not confuse task completion with outcome delivery.</li>
    <li>Do not wait until blocked to communicate risk.</li>
    <li>Do not rely on manager rescue for routine execution gaps.</li>
  </ul>
  <p>Ownership means reducing uncertainty for everyone around you.</p>
</Callout>

## Done Criteria

This step is complete when the engineer can independently drive medium-complexity work from planning through stable release.
