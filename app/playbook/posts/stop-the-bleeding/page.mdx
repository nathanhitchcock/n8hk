With a baseline in place, you can now act. Step 3 is about removing the biggest sources of drag quickly and visibly.

You are not trying to perfect the system yet. You are trying to reduce failure demand and restore execution confidence.

## Objective

Eliminate the top two or three recurring blockers that create the most team friction and stakeholder pain.

## Choose What to Fix

Pick interventions using this filter:

- High frequency: happens every week.
- High cost: creates delay, rework, or conflict.
- High control: your team can fix it without waiting on a full org redesign.

If an issue fails one of these, defer it.

## First 30-Day Intervention Set

Start with tactical fixes that improve daily execution:

- Clarify ownership for active work and unresolved decisions.
- Remove low-value work from sprint/iteration scope.
- Introduce a blocker escalation lane with same-day triage.
- Tighten handoff standards between functions.

Each fix needs a named owner, start date, and success signal.

## Operating Cadence for This Step

Use a simple weekly loop:

1. Monday: confirm priorities and risks.
2. Midweek: review blockers and escalation actions.
3. Friday: measure outcomes and adjust next week.

Repeat for four weeks before adding major new process.

	<ul>
		<li>Do not launch broad transformation language too early.</li>
		<li>Do not stack initiatives faster than the team can absorb.</li>
		<li>Do not confuse activity with progress.</li>

## Artifacts to Ship

By end of Step 3, publish:

1. Top blocker dashboard (simple, weekly).
2. Active interventions list with owners.
3. 30-day progress summary to stakeholders.

## Done Criteria

This step is complete when:

- Blocked work drops measurably.
- Decision latency improves.
- Team confidence increases because priorities feel executable.

Momentum is a management asset. Protect it once you create it.
