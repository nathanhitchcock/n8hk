A team becomes high-performing when capability expands faster than complexity.

Step 7 is about making your team stronger at decision-making, execution, and leadership depth.

## Objective

Increase team leverage by developing skills, delegation confidence, and next-level ownership.

## Build a Capability Map

Start with a simple matrix:

- Core skills by role.
- Current confidence level.
- Desired level in 90 days.
- Development owner.

This turns vague growth conversations into operational plans.

## Design a Delegation Ladder

Use four delegation levels:

1. Observe and recommend.
2. Decide with review.
3. Decide and inform.
4. Own and coach others.

Move people up deliberately. Delegation is a capability system, not a trust gamble.

## Coaching Cadence

Install recurring growth check-ins:

- Biweekly coaching one-on-ones.
- Monthly growth review tied to real work.
- Quarterly capability progression check.

Focus on behavior change and judgment quality, not only output volume.

## Increase Leadership Depth

Identify emerging leaders early and give them:

- Scoped decisions with real consequences.
- Stakeholder exposure.
- Responsibility for one operating mechanism (quality, planning, or onboarding).

Depth prevents performance collapse when key people are unavailable.

<Callout title="Guardrails">
	<ul>
		<li>Do not confuse busyness with growth.</li>
		<li>Do not delegate accountability without authority.</li>
		<li>Do not let coaching become purely motivational with no tactical follow-through.</li>
	</ul>
	<p>Capability grows through reps, feedback, and increasing responsibility.</p>
</Callout>

## Artifacts to Ship

By end of Step 7, publish:

1. Team capability matrix.
2. Delegation ladder by role.
3. Coaching cadence standard.

## Done Criteria

This step is complete when:

- More decisions are made effectively at the edge of the team.
- Manager bottlenecks decrease.
- Team members show visible progression in ownership and judgment.

Capability is what turns short-term wins into long-term performance.
