Once cadence and ownership are in place, quality becomes the multiplier.

Step 6 is about creating standards that survive pressure and reducing rework through better upfront clarity.

## Objective

Increase first-pass quality by defining standards, tightening review loops, and closing recurring failure patterns.

## Define "Good" Explicitly

Quality fails when "good" is implicit.

Create a team-level definition of done that includes:

- Functional acceptance criteria.
- Non-functional quality criteria.
- Operational readiness (monitoring, runbooks, rollback path).
- Documentation expectations.

If quality is not codified, it is negotiated under deadline stress.

## Improve Review Systems

Strengthen review quality through structure:

- Use checklists for recurring high-risk work.
- Require rationale in approvals, not just approval stamps.
- Track recurring review comments and fix at source.

Reviews should produce learning, not just gatekeeping.

## Close the Rework Loop

For each major rework event, ask:

- Which standard was missing or unclear?
- Which signal could have surfaced this earlier?
- What process adjustment prevents recurrence?

Turn mistakes into system improvements, not one-off corrections.

## Quality Metrics That Matter

Track:

- Rework ratio.
- Escaped defect rate.
- First-pass acceptance rate.
- Recovery time from incidents.

These measures expose whether quality is improving or merely appearing stable.

<Callout title="Guardrails">
	<ul>
		<li>Do not trade speed for polish everywhere.</li>
		<li>Do not let standards become bureaucracy.</li>
		<li>Do not rely only on heroic individuals to uphold quality.</li>
	</ul>
	<p>Quality should be systemic, not personality-dependent.</p>
</Callout>

## Artifacts to Ship

By end of Step 6, publish:

1. Team definition of done.
2. Review checklist templates.
3. Rework prevention log.

## Done Criteria

This step is complete when:

- First-pass quality improves.
- Rework and escaped defects decline.
- Team standards are followed consistently without constant manager intervention.

High quality lowers operating cost over time.
