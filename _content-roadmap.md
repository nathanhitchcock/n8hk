# Content Roadmap

Stories I want to write. Notes to myself so I don't forget the angle.

---

## Work (/work)

- [ ] **The NOC Relocation** (Aviat Networks, ~2010)
  Write about moving the NOC from Raleigh to San Antonio while keeping everything live. Three people, no downtime, customers had no idea it was happening. What does it actually take to pull that off?

- [ ] **The Compliance Machine** (Rackspace, 2018–2020)
  Write about building continuous compliance for FedRAMP, PCI, SOX, HITRUST, NIST. We passed first-year audits because we made it observable — not because we worked harder. That's the angle.

- [ ] **The Pod Model** (Network to Code, 2022–2024)
  Write about how we went from 3–5 clients per pod to 14 without burning anyone out. The insight was tightening interfaces, not adding headcount. Scope honesty as a scaling strategy.

- [ ] **Cloud Ops Launch** (Network to Code, 2023–2024)
  Write about taking a new service line from zero to core growth pillar inside an existing company. The internal friction, the GTM decisions, what "portfolio rationalization" actually looks like in practice.

## Blog (/blog)

- [ ] **On Knowing Where Failures Cascade**
  The Rackspace mindset: the job wasn't knowing every device, it was knowing where a failure would propagate. What does that pattern recognition look like at 40,000 nodes, and how do you build a team that develops it?

- [ ] **The Cost of Saying No to Revenue**
  What it actually means to rationalize a service portfolio — removing offerings that can't be delivered reliably. The internal resistance you'll face and why it's the right call anyway.
